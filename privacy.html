Privacy Policy

Effective date: 1 March 2026  |  Last updated: 10 April 2026

The short version: Ekurhuleni Underground does not sell your data, does not run advertising networks, and collects only what is necessary for the publication to function. This policy explains what we collect, why, and what your rights are under South African law.
1. Who We Are

Ekurhuleni Underground is published by 1906 Reblabs, an independent publishing operation based in South Africa. In terms of the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA), 1906 Reblabs is the responsible party for personal information processed in connection with this website and publication.

2. What Information We Collect

We collect the minimum information necessary to operate the publication. Specifically:

  • Automatically collected data. When you visit this website, your browser transmits standard technical information to our hosting provider (GitHub Pages). This may include your IP address, browser type, referring page, and time of visit. This data is processed by GitHub in accordance with their own privacy policy and is used for infrastructure security and performance purposes only.
  • Analytics data. If we use anonymised analytics tools to understand how our content is read, we do so without collecting personally identifiable information. We do not use tools that track individual users across websites.
  • Correspondence. If you contact us directly — for example, to submit a tip or make an inquiry — we retain your contact details and the content of your message for the purpose of responding to you. We do not add you to any distribution list without your explicit consent.

We do not collect names, email addresses, or any other personal information simply by virtue of you reading our publication.

3. Cookies and Tracking

This website is hosted on GitHub Pages, a static hosting platform. We do not place advertising cookies, behavioural tracking cookies, or marketing pixels on your device. If your browser stores any cookies from this domain, they are placed by your browser or by GitHub's infrastructure for functional purposes such as security or session management — not by us for tracking or advertising purposes.

We do not participate in any advertising network, affiliate marketing scheme, or third-party data enrichment program.

4. How We Use Information

Any personal information we hold is used only for the following purposes:

  • Responding to correspondence or inquiries you have initiated.
  • Understanding, in aggregate and anonymised form, how our content is being read so that we can improve its quality and accessibility.
  • Complying with legal obligations under South African law.

We do not use personal information for profiling, automated decision-making, or any commercial purpose unrelated to the operation of the publication.

5. Sharing of Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information to any third party under any circumstances. We do not share personal information with advertisers, data brokers, or marketing organisations. The only circumstances in which we would disclose personal information to a third party are:

  • Where required by law, court order, or binding directive from a competent South African authority.
  • Where necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of 1906 Reblabs, its contributors, or the public, and where disclosure is proportionate and legally justified.
6. Your Rights Under POPIA

The Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 gives you specific rights in relation to personal information that we hold about you. These include:

  • The right to access personal information we hold about you.
  • The right to correct or delete personal information that is inaccurate, excessive, or no longer necessary.
  • The right to object to the processing of your personal information on reasonable grounds.
  • The right to complain to the Information Regulator of South Africa if you believe your rights under POPIA have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us directly. We will respond within a reasonable period and, where required by law, within the timeframes specified by POPIA.

The Information Regulator of South Africa can be reached at inforeg.org.za.

7. Data Security

This website is served over HTTPS and hosted on GitHub Pages, which maintains industry-standard infrastructure security practices. We do not store personal information on our own servers beyond what is held in email correspondence. We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect any personal information we hold against unauthorised access, disclosure, or destruction.

8. Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected. Correspondence is typically retained for as long as the subject matter of the correspondence remains relevant. If you request deletion of personal information we hold about you, we will do so unless we are required by law to retain it.

9. Children's Privacy

This publication is not directed at children under the age of 18, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from minors. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal information from a minor, we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or in applicable law. We will update the effective date at the top of this page when we do so. Continued use of this website after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy. We encourage you to review this page periodically.

11. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or about how we handle your personal information, please contact us through the publication website. We are committed to resolving privacy concerns fairly and promptly.
